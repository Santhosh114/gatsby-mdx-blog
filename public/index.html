Bio

I am a data visualization engineer passionate about developing visual models that exert least perceptual friction.
Have spent most of my recent years building data-driven products tailor-made to support self-containing exploratory analysis & infographics for visual storytelling. My design process values intuition over gratuitous extravagance.

I received my masters degree in Data Science from Indiana University , Bloomington where I was introduced to info-vis and d3, with which I ended up spending most of my time, fascinated by visual encodings. I would like to present this wheel calandar inspired chart of time spent on various domains within data science, starting from my masters upto work.


Besides data visualization, I indulge myself in the following:

  • Aquascaping full/semi-planted fresh water tanks & keeping schooling fishes
  • Exploring the intricacies of horology, specifically automatic movements
  • Playing chess, mostly Blits & Bullet. Feel free to challenge me at Chess.com
